06 — Undergraduate Research

Published Study

Thesis · Best Paper 2025
Zero-Inflated Beta, Tobit, and Latent Growth Mixture Models for Analyzing GenAI Usage Trends and Trajectories
Jerahmeel S. Hipolito, Angeli D. Bergonio,
Carina Jean A. Damasco, Lance Aron O. Javier,
Dr. Marco C. Mandap
Bulacan State University · College of Science
Presented at 16th National Convention on Statistics, 2025
Methods: ZIB · Tobit · LGMM

Abstract

With the rise of GenAI, its increasing use in academia has become a cause for concern regarding research integrity. However, studies analyzing its usage trends, whether in global or local contexts and even in Bulacan State University (BulSU), are still limited. This study analyzes GenAI usage trends in BulSU from 2013 to 2023, analyzing 6,343 research papers authored by students and detected 262 AI-flagged papers using Quillbot Premium. Due to the presence of excess zeroes in the data, Zero-Inflated Beta and Tobit models were applied to trend analysis, while Latent Growth Mixture Models (LGMM) determined distinct class trajectories. By 2023, the expected AI scores of Quadratic Tobit were censored at 0% for individual-level and reached 33.36% for discipline-level. Additionally, the trend of AI scores behaves in a way that suggests a rapid move toward full adoption (censored at 100%) by 2026 for both levels, based on projections extrapolated from the 2013–2023 observed data. Furthermore, the Quadratic LGMM classified disciplines into three latent class groups and are described based on Roger's Innovation Diffusion Theory with Innovators and Early Adopters, Early and Late Majority, and Laggards, where Early and Late Majority show a trend behavior that reaches GenAI usage censored at 100% by year 2026, which overtakes Innovators and Early Adopters with only 87.33%. The findings show that GenAI usage in academic research shows a clear upward trend while varying from disciplines. As GenAI may become fully integrated in the coming years based on the direction of the current trend, the study highlights the need for ethical GenAI usage while encouraging innovation in research.
